Parenting Styles

Styles of Parenting explained by N. McBride

Authoritarian

Child has no input. Parents have complete say over the child’s decisions. Children who have authoritarian parents often struggle to develop the skill and ability to make decisions for themselves.

Authoritative

Child has input, but the parents have the final say. Children who have authoritative parents often have a well-developed sense of self-esteem and confidence in decision-making, because they have been allowed to practice those skills with support.

Permissive

Child has the final say. If the parent does not like the decisions the child is making, they will express frustration and make an attempt to correct the behavior without giving punishments. If their attempts are unsuccessful, they will give up and allow the child to deal with the consequences of his or her choices.

Uninvolved

Parents are typically egocentric in their child-rearing, and are uncommitted to their role as a parent, and will remain distant from their children.
